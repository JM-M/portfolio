## The problem

Solar adoption in Nigeria is growing, but the buying process is still confusing for most people. Customers don't know what equipment they need, how much it costs, or what the installation process looks like. Greeniilde had a strong service (design, supply, and full installation of solar systems) but no clear way to communicate it online or guide potential customers from curiosity to a consultation.

## How I approached it

Solar is a high-consideration purchase. People don't impulse-buy a panel system. So the site needed to educate first, build trust, and then make it easy to take the next step.

**Structured around the buyer's journey.** Instead of leading with product specs, I organized the site around the questions people actually have: What solutions exist for my situation? What equipment is involved? What does the process look like? How much will it cost me over time? Each section answers one of these questions clearly, moving the visitor closer to a decision without overwhelming them.

**Product pages that inform, not overwhelm.** The catalog covers panels, inverters, batteries, and irrigation pumps. Each product page focuses on what matters to a non-technical buyer: what it does, why it's reliable, and how it fits into a full system. Technical specs are there for those who want them, but they're not the lead.

**Case studies and social proof.** I built a case studies section that showcases real installations with measurable results. Combined with the company's track record (450+ installations, 99.2% uptime), this gives visitors confidence that they're dealing with a team that delivers.

**Performance for real-world conditions.** Many users access the site on mobile over varying network speeds. I focused on fast load times through server-side rendering and optimized asset delivery, because a slow site in this market means a lost lead.


## Where it landed

The site gives Greeniilde a professional digital presence that does more than look good. It educates potential customers, builds credibility through real results, and makes it straightforward to move from browsing to a consultation. It works as well on a phone in Lagos as it does on a desktop.
